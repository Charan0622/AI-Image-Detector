The problem

AI-generated images are everywhere. Detectors aren't.

Three forces colliding

  • Generative models keep getting better
  • Detectors overfit to specific generators
  • Real-world images differ from benchmark data
A model can score 96% on the academic benchmark and still mislabel half of casual smartphone photos as AI-generated.

SPECTRA 06 / 14
Deployment gap

A 96% benchmark detector calls 44% of smartphone photos AI.

Held-out · 100 picsum smartphones + 17 modern AI
44%
Real-photo false-positive rate

On a held-out set of casual smartphone photos — images with HDR tonemapping, sensor noise, and Instagram-style colour grading — the same model that scores 0.994 AUC on the benchmark falsely flags 44 of every 100 real photos as AI-generated.

The benchmark hides the gap because its "real" class is ImageNet-style, not smartphone-style.

SPECTRA 07 / 14
The fix

Smartphone-aesthetic augmentation + 632 picsum photos

SmartphoneAesthetic

  • ColorJitter (brightness, contrast, saturation, hue)
  • Random gamma 0.7–1.4 (HDR tonemapping)
  • Sensor read-noise σ ≤ 4/255
  • ±1 px chromatic aberration
  • Double-JPEG re-encode (social media pipeline)

Plus

632 picsum photos appended to training set.
Warm fine-tune for 3 epochs at 1/5× LR.

SPECTRA 08 / 14
Negative findings

What we tried and rejected

🚫
Ensemble of all 5 heads
−1.5 points robust AUC vs the best single model. All heads share the frozen CLIP backbone, so errors are correlated. Averaging adds the weaker models' mistakes without decorrelation benefit.
🚫
Test-time augmentation (horizontal flip)
+0.001 AUC for 2× inference cost. Below noise floor. The model already has flip invariance through its augmentation training.
✓
Temperature scaling
ECE drops 2-4×. Hybrid was overconfident at T=2.45 (a 0.99 confidence was a 0.75 honest probability). Deployed.
SPECTRA 09 / 14
Limits

Modern generators we don't have data for

Flux, Imagen 3, Midjourney v6+, Gemini Nano-Banana — released after our training data.

Production strategy

Ensemble at inference, not at training.

Our 5 custom heads remain available for the research narrative (clean GenImage benchmark, robustness ablation, calibration, OOD).

For deployment we route through a public HuggingFace ViT-base detector haywoodsloan/ai-image-detector-deploy that scores 100% on smartphone photos and 100% on Pollinations modern AI — exactly the two failure modes our heads have.

Honest framing: we built and benchmarked five architectures; for production we ensemble with a public SOTA detector that covers modern generators we couldn't source training data for.

SPECTRA 12 / 14
Recap

Three contributions

1
Five-model controlled ablation
Frequency branch is the largest gain (+0.039 AUC). Robustness aug is a Pareto improvement under degradation. Combined attention + aug actually hurts generalisation.
2
Quantified deployment gap + remediation
44% real-photo FPR on smartphone images, hidden by the GenImage benchmark. SmartphoneAesthetic + 632 picsum injection drops FPR to 7%.
3
Production-ready, honest interface
FastAPI + Apple-HIG React. Calibrated probabilities, OOD score, five-band verdict. Public-detector fallback for 2024+ generators we couldn't source data for.
